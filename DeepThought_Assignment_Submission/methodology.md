# Methodology: Ahmedabad Performance Chemicals - Part A

## Choice

- City: Ahmedabad operating cluster
- Segment: Basket A - Performance chemicals

I treated "Ahmedabad" as the real operating cluster around Ahmedabad city: Vatva, Odhav, Naroda, Bavla, Sanand, and nearby Ahmedabad-linked chemical corridors. I did not count companies where Ahmedabad was only a sales office.

## Research Process

1. Started from Ahmedabad's strongest performance-chemical subclusters:
   - Phthalocyanine and organic pigments
   - Reactive, acid and direct dyes
   - Textile auxiliaries and water-treatment chemicals
   - Coating resins and engineered polymer systems

2. Checked the two gates first:
   - E1 Producer: must manufacture products or have real technical operating infrastructure.
   - E2 Accessible: must have India and preferably Ahmedabad-cluster operational presence.

3. Scored each company on the assignment's Federer criteria:
   - C3 Differentiated: 20
   - C4 Decision-maker quality: 15
   - C5 Growing sector: 15
   - C6 Growth signals: 15
   - C7 Systems maturity: 20
   - C8 Leadership succession: 15

4. Used the assignment's weak/moderate/strong approach:
   - Weak = 0
   - Moderate = half weight, rounded to 8 for 15-point criteria
   - Strong = full weight

## Sources Used

- Company websites for product, plant, certification, R&D and location evidence.
- Screener for listed-company revenue, public filings and promoter context.
- Direct website reachability checks for candidate validation.
- Public web search for company discovery and false-positive screening.

## How To Use The CSV

The CSV has 25 rows because the assignment asks for a 25-company spreadsheet, but the rows are intentionally marked by `Submit Priority`.

- `Use`: strongest rows to keep.
- `Use after verification`: relevant targets, but verify revenue, leadership or plant evidence before upload.
- `Backup`: weak or incomplete evidence; keep only if more sources confirm them.
- `Do not use`: false positives retained to show research judgment, not final targets.

If time is short, submit only after replacing `Do not use` rows with more verified SMEs from CHEMEXCIL, GIDC Vatva/Naroda lists, GDMA, MCA/Zauba/Tofler and trade directories. The false positives are useful in a fail-list tab or methodology appendix because they show ICP judgment.

## Key Learnings

Ahmedabad has a deep colorant and performance-chemicals ecosystem, but many famous companies fail DeepThought's ICP:

- Too large: Jay Chemical, Bodal, Meghmani, likely Kiri/AksharChem/Asahi depending on current revenue.
- Wrong city: Gujarat Polysol is a strong performance-chemicals company but not Ahmedabad.
- Wrong ownership/city fit: Heubach is technically strong but subsidiary/acquired and not cleanly Ahmedabad-promoter-led.

The best current target profile is not the largest pigment/dye exporter. It is the mid-sized operator-led manufacturer with visible process systems, certifications, plant infrastructure and a promoter/technical leader still close to operations.
